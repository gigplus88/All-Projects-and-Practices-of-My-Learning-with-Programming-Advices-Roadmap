    <div class="demo-block">I am <code>display: block</code> (full width).</div>
    <p>Converted (inline):</p>
    <div class="demo-inline-convert">I am <code>display: inline</code></div>
    <div class="demo-inline-convert">so I flow side by side</div>
    <p class="note">Notice how the inline elements sit on the same line, only as wide as their content.</p>
  </section>


   <section>
    <h2>2) Nav Menu (inline-block on <li>)</h2>
    <nav>
      <ul>
        <li><a href="#">Home</a></li>
        <li><a href="#">About</a></li>
        <li><a href="#">Contact</a></li>
      </ul>
    </nav>
    <p class="note">Each `<li>` is <code>display: inline-block</code>, allowing padding and margins without breaking the line.</p>
  </section>

   
    <section>
        <h2>3) Button Styling: inline vs inline-block</h2>
        <p>
        <button class="inline">Inline Button</button>
        <button class="inline-block">Inline-Block Button</button>
        </p>
        <p class="note">
        The <code>inline</code> button cannot have its width/height adjusted independently,  
        whereas the <code>inline-block</code> button respects width/height and margins.
        </p>
